Overview

String quartet source separation poses unique challenges due to overlapping fundamental frequencies, similar timbres, and complex acoustic environments inherent in chamber music. Traditional methods typically use single-channel input, which fails to leverage the spatial information embedded in the music, resulting in suboptimal separation performance. In this paper, we present a novel approach that enhances string quartet separation by incorporating positional cues into a conditional modulation network. To evaluate our method, we create a specialized dataset of string quartet recordings. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach effectively separates the individual instruments and highlights the importance of positional encoding in improving separation quality.
